About

I am a computational chemist and biophysicist with more than 10 years of interdisciplinary research experience in molecular dynamics simulations, protein modeling, enhanced sampling, free energy calculations, and computer-aided drug design. I integrate simulation with experimental techniques including solid-state NMR, solution NMR, and cryo-EM to build and validate molecular-level models.

I develop automated computational workflows and open tools for protein property prediction, membrane association analysis, and phase-separation modeling. I am author of 28 peer-reviewed articles and 2 book chapters, with contributions spanning membrane proteins, protein–drug interactions, and disease-linked aggregation.

Research Interests

Protein Structure & Dynamics

Membrane protein modeling, integrative structure determination with NMR restraints, voltage-dependent MD, and AlphaFold-guided workflows.

Intrinsically Disordered Proteins

Residue-level membrane-association prediction, sequence determinants of binding, and automated trajectory analysis pipelines.

Biomolecular Condensates

Phase separation, binodal prediction, interfacial tension, and sequence–function relationships in peptide and protein condensates.

AI & Predictive Modeling

Machine learning for property prediction, representation learning from simulations, Python/JAX pipelines, and open computational tools.

Protein–Ligand Interactions

Virtual screening, docking, pharmacophore modeling, QSAR, and structure-based drug design for therapeutic discovery.

Experimental Integration

Collaborative projects linking computational predictions with NMR, cell-based assays, and mechanistic validation.
